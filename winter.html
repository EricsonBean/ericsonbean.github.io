Hurricane Ridge

Stay:

Heart of the Hills Campground at the bottom of Hurricane Ridge Road is open year around first come, first serve with 105 campsites and a fee of $24. No electrical hook ups.

Do:

Hurricane Ridge is the primary location for skiing in the Olympics due to its accessibility. Park at the top by the visitor center to start tours to Hurricane Ridge and Obstruction Point. The traverse to Hurricane Hill is 700 ft up, not much. Add laps by skiing down and back up to the ridge where appealing. To the west, traverse along the closed obstruction point road for 7.5 miles to the foot of Obstruction Peak. Start a steeper climb to the top or traverse around to head towards Elk Mountain. Tour up to the East or West of Mount Angeles starting from pull out parking prior to the top of road.

Eat:

Bring own, no town nearby!

Mount Ellinor

Stay:

Heart of the Hills Campground at the bottom Hurricane Ridge Road is open year around first come, first serve with 105 campsites and a fee of $24. No electrical hook ups.

Do:

Mount Ellinor offers a chute descent with views of Lake Cushman and the Olympic Mountains. It is most common to park at the lower trailhead and start your tour from there following the hiking trail up. On the way down, some opt to return to the trailhead via the forest road from the upper trailhead in order to avoid skiing through shrub.

Eat:

Bring own, no town nearby!

Paradise

Stay:

Heart of the Hills Campground at the bottom Hurricane Ridge Road is open year around first come,firse serve with 105 campsites and a fee of $24. No electrical hook ups.

Do:

Ranier National Park keeps the road to Paradise open pending day of conditions from 9-4. Make sure to always check there twitter updates day prior and morning of. AFter heavy snowfall, the road to Paradise may be delayed until noon or not open at all. Skin up to Camp Muir for a heck of a workout and guarnateed snow cover most of the year. Winter and Spring Conditions will often be windy, possibly whiteout. This will most likely be an icy, bumpy descent. Also accessible from Paradise is the Tatoosh Range, mainly Lane's peak. Lane's peak has two popular couloirs to boot up and ski down, Fly and Zipper Couloirs. Both are narrow and around 45 deg. Fly is more steep but wider. For a more mild tour, head up to the ski bason to the east of Lane Peak. For an different tour in the Tatoosh Range, travers over towards Reflection Lake along the closed Steven Canyons Road. Begin your tour at the pinnacle Peak Trailhead ascending the ridge towards Pinnacle and Castle. Skin up the saddle petween the Peaks or on either side. More milder terrain would be more east of Castle Peak.
Bring own, no town nearby!
