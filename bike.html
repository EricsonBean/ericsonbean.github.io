Hoh Forest

Stay:

Coppermine Bottom and Clearwater Campgrounds are first come first serve and free with a Discover Pass. The gravel rides shown start from those campgrounds. There is also dispersed camping on Upper Hoh Road before you reach Olympic National Park.

Do:

The rides shown are taken mostly from a local gravel race. The rides will have several small hills but not too much total elevation, allowing for some longer rides. Unsure how scenic the rides will be.

Eat:

Bring own, no town nearby!

Wynochee Lake

Stay:

Coho Campground is on the lake and at start of the gravel rides. Half of the sites are resevable where the other are first come for $25. There are supposedly several dispersed camping sites on the north end of the road coming from Wynochee lake road. Satsop Campground is a first come first serve campground nearby.

Do:

Ride around the lake for an easy, flat ride. Add Forest Road 2312 for 18 miles and ~2600 feet of climbing. For an epic loop, continue on Forest Road 2204 along the Humptulips river then onto Forest Road 2404 as it takes you back across the river. These loops are part of a local gravel race. From there, can add more gravel with a small hill to get back to the lake or return on paved roads. For the second day, ride to the west along Satsop river or spoon creek. These routes are shown to have a lot of strava and Ridewithme heatmap.

Eat:

Bring own, no town nearby!

Mount Muller

Stay:

Klahoya and Fairholme campgrounds are both near the campsite, bth will be busy and take reservations. Bear Creek campground is a little farter down 101 to the east but is free and frist come, first serve

Do:

Mount Muller singetrack requires some steep climbing, but will have incredible views at the top. There is a possible Mount Muller gravel route on East or West Twin River Road. West Twin River Raoad has more heatmap data.

Eat:

Bring own, no town nearby!
